The Issues

These are a few key issues I'd like to address

Reasonable Pricing

I believe that the United States lags behind the rest of the world in a significant way: we do not include the sales tax in our listed prices. I would advocate for an ordinance in the city of West Lafayette that would require businesses to display a price that includes all applicable taxes on any goods available for purchase. This would have no impact on the way businesses operate aside from changing the displays and signage. The receipts would still include the product price and the tax separately for the convenience of the consumer.

A Counter-Clockwise Bus Loop

Like a great many of you, I have known the frustration of deciding between taking the bus and walking to class in sub-zero and sub-optimal conditions. Why, then, do the current bus loops only proceed in a clockwise fashion? Why must we go all the way around campus to get to a location which would be far closer if we went the other way? I think this is an easily resolved issue, with benefits for students and faculty alike. I will work with CityBus toward a new loop to proceed along a route similar to the existing Silver Loop, but  in the opposite direction. This is prudent, inexpensive, and necessary.

Drainage

The issue of drainage encompasses two areas of concern: drainage on campus, and drainage into the Wabash. With respect to the former, the numerous persistent puddles (or perhaps, small lakes) after a rainstorm or a hefty snowfall result in a great inconvenience to students. Ideally, if we could proceed with installing better-designed drainage systems, we could channel the flow of the water into our drains. Heck, if the Romans could do it, so can we!

The other issue concerns the dumping of raw sewage into the Wabash. I was shocked to find out that the city’s sewage system is ill-equipped to handle large rainstorms and, in the event of such a storm, there are not enough facilities to process the sewage and the rainwater. What happens? You guessed it, the raw, untreated sewage flows straight into our river! By expanding our overflow capacity at the municipal level. We can work toward lowering the environmental impact of these policies.

Student Involvement

Our city government must be transparent and accessible to the people. I will actively work toward ensuring that students and their ideas are welcome inputs in our local government. I’ll be very honest here, most students don’t really care about politics. We are only here for four years, right? Why should we care? Quite simply, politics runs the world and, to that end, it is vital for students to feel involved as a part of the political process. Students should feel as if they are crucial members of the community and that their voice matters. With me, it will. I am always willing to lend my ear to a new plan or a new proposal, provided that it can be sustainably funded and have long-term benefits.  Another thing to consider is the matter of legacy. Students, what do you want to leave behind for the coming generations of Boilermakers? I can help us work toward permanent improvements, especially with respect to the State Street redesign, to Purdue and to West Lafayette that will stand for years to come.

Business

These are changing times for the Greater Lafayette Area. The recent development of the Anvil and other such incubators bodes well for the future of entrepreneurship in the area. Increasing funding and the new GE factory seem to suggest that manufacturing is here to stay. I believe that a balanced economy contains a healthy mix of agriculture, manufacturing, services, and high-tech startups. To this end, I believe that attracting new business is crucial for the continued health and growth of West Lafayette. By giving businesses incentives to come here and hire locally, we can build a more prosperous and wealthy community. Increasing student retention is also very important; although we produce some of the most talented scientists, engineers, agricultural, and technical workers in the country, very few of them stay to enrich the community after graduation. By attracting more business, we can ensure more jobs for students during and after their time at Purdue. Initiatives like Purdue's research park are a great step in this direction, but increased enthusiasm at the city council level would only aid our growth.

State Street Master Plan

I wholly endorse the larger aims of the State Street Master Plan, as I believe that it has the potential to entirely reinvigorate our downtown area. In this regard, my aim is to consolidate student feedback to ensure that the plans and the construction proceed in a fashion that is most amicable for both students, the community, and local businesses. What we make here will last for generations, so I want to make sure that we build it responsibly and with comprehensive student input.
